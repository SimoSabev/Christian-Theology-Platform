## Quick Reference: All Arguments By Category

### 🌌 Cosmological Arguments
1. **The Kalam Cosmological Argument** — From the impossibility of an infinite past and the scientific evidence for a beginning, concluding a transcendent personal cause.
2. **The Leibnizian Contingency Argument** — From the contingency of the universe and the Principle of Sufficient Reason to a Necessary Being.
3. **Aquinas's Five Ways** — Five converging lines of reasoning: Motion, Efficient Causation, Contingency, Degrees of Perfection, and Teleology.
4. **The Argument from the Applicability of Mathematics** — From the "unreasonable effectiveness of mathematics" in describing physical reality.

### 💭 Ontological Arguments
1. **Anselm's Classical Ontological Argument** — From the concept of "that than which nothing greater can be conceived" to its necessary real existence.
2. **Descartes' Ontological Argument** — From the clear and distinct idea of a supremely perfect being, whose essence necessarily includes existence.
3. **Plantinga's Modal Ontological Argument** — From the mere possibility of a Maximally Great Being to its actual necessary existence (in S5 modal logic).
4. **Maydole's Modal Perfection Argument** — A strengthened version proving the logical compatibility of all perfections.
5. **Gödel's Ontological Proof** — A formal logical proof using the theory of positive properties, verified by computer.

### 🔬 Teleological Arguments
1. **The Fine-Tuning Argument** — From the extraordinary calibration of physical constants to intelligent design.
2. **The Argument from Biological Information** — From the specified complex information in DNA to an intelligent source.
3. **The Argument from Irreducible Complexity** — From molecular machines that cannot be built incrementally to design.
4. **The Argument from Natural Law** — From the law-governed nature of the universe to a rational Lawgiver.
5. **The Argument from Beauty** — From the gratuitous beauty of the cosmos to a Creator who delights in beauty.

### ⚖️ Moral Arguments
1. **The Moral Argument** — From the existence of objective moral values and duties to God as their foundation.
2. **The Euthyphro Solution** — God's nature IS the standard of goodness (the Third Option).
3. **The Argument from Conscience** — From the authoritative voice of conscience to a personal moral Lawgiver.
4. **The Argument from Human Dignity** — From universal human rights to the *imago Dei* as their foundation.
5. **The Problem of Evil Reversed** — The very concept of "evil" presupposes the objective moral standard that only God provides.

### 📜 Historical Evidence
1. **The Minimal Facts Argument for the Resurrection** — Using only scholar-accepted facts to establish the Resurrection as the best explanation.
2. **The Reliability of the New Testament** — Manuscript evidence, early dating, eyewitness testimony, archaeological confirmation.
3. **The Argument from the Origin of Christianity** — The unprecedented "mutation" in resurrection belief demands explanation.
4. **Non-Christian Sources** — Tacitus, Josephus, Pliny, Lucian, the Talmud, Mara bar Serapion.
5. **The Argument from Prophecy** — Messianic prophecies fulfilled in Jesus, verified by the Dead Sea Scrolls.
